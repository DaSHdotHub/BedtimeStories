Puss In Boots

Puss in Boots



In a land where tales unfold,
A cat in boots, brave and bold,
His cunning plan, masterfully told,
Turned his master's life to gold.

With a sly smile and a clever scheme,
Puss defeated ogre's dream.
In a castle, as if by a beam,
He wove reality from seam.

The king, impressed by such feat,
Marquis of Carabas he did greet.
Puss's tricks were quite neat,
In a dance of deception sweet.

Thus, the cat with wit so grand,
Earned his master vast land.
In the end, they took a stand,
Side by side, hand in hand.

From humble beginnings to royal halls,
Puss in boots, he enthralls.
Through each challenge and brawl,
His courage never falls.

So ends the tale of a cat so clever,
His legend lives on forever.
In the hearts of those who endeavor,
Puss's spirit fades never.

Snow White

Snow White And The Seven Dwarfs



In a forest deep and wide,
Seven dwarfs did there abide,
With hearts as pure as morning tide,
A princess came to hide.

Mirror's truth, a queen did fear,
Snow White's beauty, crystal clear.
Beneath the moon's soft, silvery sphere,
In slumber, she lay near.

An apple red, with poison laced,
In dreams, a single kiss was placed,
A prince's love, not a moment's waste,
Woke her with gentle grace.

Through trials dark, and friendship rare,
Their story sings through the forest air,
A tale of love, beyond compare,
In hearts forever fair.

Through woods they roam, a joyous band,
Hand in hand, through enchanted land,
In the cottage small, by friendship grand,
Together they make their stand.

In the end, the queen's defeat,
Revealed by truth, no more deceit.
In a kingdom where hearts entreat,
Love and kindness meet.

So ends the tale of Snow White fair,
With love and joy beyond compare.
In every heart, a song to share,
Of a princess, kind and rare.

Hansel and Gretel

Hansel and Gretel



In a forest deep, where shadows creep,
Hansel and Gretel, lost, began to weep.
Their stepmother's plan, so harsh and steep,
Left them alone, in woods so bleak and deep.

With pebbles white, Hansel's clever trail,
Hoping to escape, not to fail.
But as night fell, their faces pale,
In the dark woods, their hopes frail.

Then, a house of candy, a sugary sight,
Windows of sugar, a delight.
But inside, a witch, her heart not right,
Planned a feast in the moon's pale light.

Hansel trapped in a cage so tight,
Gretel feared through the dark night.
But with her wit and all her might,
She planned their escape, away from fright.

Outwitting the witch, with a trick so bold,
Pushing her into the oven's hold.
Free at last, brave and bold,
They ran from the house, no longer controlled.

Back through the woods, the siblings raced,
To their father's arms, they embraced.
With the wicked stepmother now displaced,
In their humble home, love they faced.

Their days were filled with joy anew,
Under skies so wide and blue.
In every heart, hope grew,
As Hansel and Gretel's story flew.

A tale of courage, strong and true,
Proving what love and bravery can do.
In the darkest times, they made it through,
Together, their bond forever grew.
